Frequently Asked Questions - Diabetic Diet

Q: What should be the ideal daily carbohydrate intake for a person with diabetes?

A: The ideal daily carbohydrate intake varies, but it's generally recommended to focus on complex carbohydrates and monitor blood glucose levels.

Q: Are there specific fruits that are better for people with diabetes?

A: Yes, fruits with lower glycemic index values, such as berries and melons, are generally better choices. Moderation is key.

Q: Can I include sweets in my diet occasionally if I have diabetes?

A: Occasional treats are allowed, but it's important to monitor portion sizes and maintain overall control of carbohydrate intake.

Q: What role does fiber play in a diabetic diet?

A: Fiber helps control blood sugar levels by slowing down the absorption of sugar. It's recommended to include fiber-rich foods like whole grains and vegetables.

Q: Should I follow a specific meal timing schedule?

A: Consistent meal timing can help regulate blood sugar levels. It's often recommended to eat smaller, balanced meals throughout the day.

Q: Is it necessary to count calories on a diabetic diet?

A: While calorie counting is not the primary focus, portion control and choosing nutrient-dense foods are essential for managing diabetes.

Q: How does protein intake affect blood sugar levels?

A: Protein has a minimal effect on blood sugar levels. Including lean protein sources can contribute to a balanced diabetic diet.

Q: Are there specific cooking methods recommended for a diabetic diet?

A: Healthier cooking methods, such as baking, grilling, and steaming, are recommended for a diabetic diet to reduce added fats and oils.
